# Coverage

## What Is Included

- **Organic certification standards** from Soil Association, OF&G, and EU Baseline (Regulation 2018/848 retained in UK law): arable, dairy, beef/sheep, poultry, horticulture, processing
- **Permitted inputs**: fertilisers (Annex I), plant protection products (Annex II), feed additives (Annex III) with conditions, limits, and derogation status
- **Conversion rules**: timelines for arable, permanent grassland, permanent crops, cattle, sheep/pigs, poultry with marketing and support options
- **Cover crop guidance**: 12 species/mixes with N fixation rates, biomass yields, sowing windows, destruction methods, and best following crops
- **Biodiversity net gain**: 8 habitat types with BNG unit values, creation costs, management obligations, and grant availability
- **Soil health indicators**: 8 indicators with target ranges, measurement methods, management practices, and improvement timelines

## What Is NOT Included

- **Scottish-specific organic advice** -- SRUC equivalents are not yet ingested
- **Northern Ireland** -- NI follows separate guidance (CAFRE) within the same EU-retained regulation
- **Aquaculture organic standards** -- Marine and freshwater aquaculture rules not included
- **Wild collection standards** -- Rules for organic wild-collected products not included
- **Real-time grant rates** -- Grant amounts are indicative; check RPA for current rates
- **Detailed BNG metric calculations** -- Use the DEFRA Biodiversity Metric 4.0 tool for formal assessments
- **Individual field recommendations** -- This is reference data, not site-specific prescription

## Known Gaps

1. Cover crop N fixation rates are expressed as typical mid-range values; actual fixation varies with soil type, climate, and management
2. BNG unit values are indicative; formal assessments require DEFRA Biodiversity Metric 4.0
3. Grant availability changes with government policy updates
4. Soil health targets are general guidance; site-specific interpretation needed

## Data Freshness

Run `check_data_freshness` to see when data was last updated. The ingestion pipeline runs on a schedule; manual triggers available via `gh workflow run ingest.yml`.
